# English Style Guide

Use for English target copy. Project `VOICE_EN.md` and the requested locale override preferences here.

## Native English structure

- State the actor and action clearly when they matter.
- Prefer strong verbs over nominalizations.
- Untangle long Russian sentences into units that fit English information flow.
- Move qualifications close to the claims they limit.
- Use articles, tense, and countability idiomatically rather than mapping Russian grammar.
- Avoid carrying Russian emphasis through unnatural inversion or repeated abstract nouns.

## Russian-to-English interference

Watch for false friends and transfer patterns:

- `actual` for «актуальный» when `current`, `relevant`, or `timely` is intended;
- `control` for «контролировать» when `manage`, `monitor`, or `check` fits;
- `realize` for «реализовать» when `implement`, `deliver`, or `build` fits;
- `complex` for «комплекс» when `suite`, `system`, `set`, or `facility` fits;
- `expertise` used as a direct replacement for every instance of «экспертиза»;
- `on the market` where `in the market` is idiomatic;
- missing articles around products, categories, and singular count nouns;
- excessive `which` clauses inherited from Russian sentence length;
- vague `it allows` constructions repeated across feature copy.

## Avoid generic AI and corporate prose

Remove or rewrite:

- `in today's fast-paced world`;
- `leverage`, `unlock`, `elevate`, `redefine`, `seamless`, `robust`, `cutting-edge`, `game-changing` when they carry no specific meaning;
- `whether you're X or Y` as a default opener;
- repeated `not only … but also …` or `it's not just X—it's Y`;
- mechanical groups of three;
- unnecessary em dashes and sentence fragments;
- summary paragraphs that repeat the same claim in new adjectives;
- empty confidence such as `the ultimate solution` or `everything you need`.

Do not ban these words when they are exact and supported. Reject automatic usage, not vocabulary itself.

## Human cadence

- Let sentence length follow emphasis and complexity.
- Use contractions when the brand and locale permit them.
- Use plain transitions; do not decorate every logical move.
- Preserve useful repetition and distinctive phrasing from the voice examples.
- Prefer one memorable image over several mixed metaphors.
- Do not imitate casual speech by inserting slang randomly.

## Locale

### en-US

- US spelling and punctuation.
- Month–day date format only when ambiguity is controlled; spell out months in public-facing copy.
- Use local units or provide both if the audience requires it.

### en-GB

- UK spelling, punctuation, and vocabulary.
- Day–month ordering; spell out months when useful.
- Avoid US sales idioms that sound imported unless the audience expects them.

Never mix variants in one asset unless reproducing an official name or quote.

## Marketing language

- Lead with a useful tension, concrete outcome, or recognizable situation.
- Connect claims to mechanism or proof.
- Prefer `what changes for the customer` over a feature inventory.
- Use urgency only when a real deadline, capacity limit, or consequence exists.
- Make CTAs specific to the next step; avoid `Learn more` when a more meaningful action is available.
- Match directness to audience sophistication. Expert audiences usually prefer precision over enthusiasm.

## Final English scan

Check for:

- Russian syntax under English vocabulary;
- false friends;
- article and preposition errors;
- inconsistent US/UK usage;
- corporate filler;
- excessive fragments, em dashes, triads, and contrast formulas;
- claims stronger than the source;
- text that is fluent but culturally or commercially wrong.
