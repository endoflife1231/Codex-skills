# Channel Playbooks

Use only the relevant section.

## Landing page

- Opening: audience situation or desired outcome, followed quickly by the product's role.
- Preserve hierarchy: headline → clarifier → proof/mechanism → benefits → objections → CTA.
- Translate page logic, not just blocks. Reorder when target-market expectations differ.
- Keep one primary CTA label consistent unless funnel stage changes.
- Avoid repeating the same value proposition in every section.

## Paid ad

- Respect platform character limits supplied by the user.
- Preserve the actual targeting premise and offer.
- One ad should usually carry one tension and one promise.
- Do not add unsupported urgency or social proof.
- In `campaign` mode, vary angles, not merely wording.

## Email

- Subject line and preview text must complement rather than duplicate each other.
- The first paragraph should justify opening without a long greeting.
- Keep one dominant CTA.
- Match sender relationship: founder, company, sales representative, community, or automated service.
- Preserve unsubscribe, legal, and transactional language conservatively.

## Social post

- Adapt to platform reading behavior without impersonating a stereotype of the platform.
- Keep a natural opening; avoid universal hook formulas.
- Break lines for comprehension, not after every sentence.
- Do not add hashtags by default. Use them only when requested or demonstrably useful.
- Preserve the author's point of view and level of certainty.

## Article or thought leadership

- Preserve thesis, evidence, and intellectual honesty.
- Rewrite examples that depend on local context only when needed.
- Do not manufacture personal experience or stronger authority.
- Use headings only where they help navigation.
- Avoid SEO phrasing that damages natural prose unless the user specifies target queries.

## Product UI

- Prioritize clarity, action, and space constraints over expressive voice.
- Preserve system state, consequence, and reversibility.
- Use consistent terminology across buttons, labels, errors, and help text.
- Do not translate placeholders, variables, or code-like tokens.
- Localize plurals, dates, currencies, and grammatical gender correctly.

## Sales message

- Preserve personalization only when grounded in supplied facts.
- Lead with relevance, not compliments.
- Keep the ask proportionate to relationship strength.
- Do not invent research about the recipient.
- Translate objection handling with the target market's expected directness.

## Press or corporate communication

- Preserve titles, attribution, dates, corporate claims, and legal nuance.
- Avoid making executives sound more casual or more grandiose than the source.
- Keep quotations semantically faithful; do not silently turn them into new campaign lines.
